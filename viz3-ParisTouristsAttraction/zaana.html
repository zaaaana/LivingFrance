Tourism Figures for Places of Interest

Paris is the # 1 most visited destination for international tourists in the world. The population of France is approximately 70 million, however the average number of international visitors annually is at least 80 million:

While the values remain consistent within the 75 - 90 million range, the dip in the number of visitors in 2016 comes as a result of the riots which occurred in 2015. This incident, however, has not stopped the city nor the country from being one of the most popular tourist destinations of the world, as visitor statistics quickly bounced back the following year.

Below are the statistics of Paris' most visited monuments and places of interest:

Notre-Dame de Paris

The Notre-Dame de Paris is the most popular tourist attraction in all of Paris, where the cathedral boasts an annual average of 13 million visitors - approximately 36 thousand visitors daily! This value, however, only accounts for about 15% of all international visitors annually. Following the blaze which occurred in early 2019, the future of the cathedral, and what this meant for tourism in Paris seemed questionable. Though primary traders who rely on the cathedral's tourism as income are directly affected by the economic impact of the fire, there is no doubt that following its reopening to the public, the cathedral will continue to share its legacy and retain its status as one of the most visited landmarks in the world.

Centre Pompidou

The Centre Pompidou opened officially to the public on 02 February, 1977, and quickly became one of the most popular cultural venues in France and worldwide. In 1997, former president Jean-Jacques Aillagon approved renovation for expansion of the centre in order to include further gallery space and improved live performance facilities. In 2000 after the official reopening, the Centre Pomidou was well received with an influx of visitors, approximately 16000 daily.

Louvre

Originally built as the Louvre castle in the 13th Century, the Louvre was converted to a museum during the French Revolution, as a way to display artwork of the nation. The Louvre is the most visited museum globally, with approximately 9 million visitors annually. It is mostly known as the home to the Mona Lisa and Venus of Milo.

Musée d'Orsay

The Musée d'Orsay is housed within the former Gare d'Orsay, which was a Beaux-Arts railway station built in the late 1800's. The museum holds mainly French paintings, sculptures and photography from the time period of 1848 - 1914. It is mostly famous for its collection of impressionist paintings, namely from Monet, Renoir and Pissarro. It is the 10th globally most visited museum, and 3rd nationally.

Eiffel Tower

Constructed in 1887, it was considered an architectural achievement upon its completion only 2 years later. The tower itself was only meant to last 20 years as a commemoration of the centennial of the French Revolution, however it was retained due to its purpose in scientific experiments, such as enabling the first radio transmissions, telecommunications and television broadcasts.

Grand Palais

The Grand Palais des Champs-Élysées was constructed in 1897 following the demolition of the Palais de l'Industrie, alongside its neighbouring Petit Palais and Pont Alexandre III. In 2000, it officialy became listed as a monument historique by the French Ministry of Culture, and is visited by approximately 1.5 million people annually.

Arc de Triomphe

The Arc de Triomphe, built in 1806, is situated in the centre of the Place Charles de Gaulle, where the juncture is formed by the 12 avenues surrounding it. The monument honours those who fought for French during the Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars, and sees approximately 2 million visitors annually.

Sacré-Cœur

The Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Paris, or more commonly known as Sacré-Cœur, it a Roman Catholic church, dedicated to the Sacred Heard of Jesus. Built in 1919, it is the 9th most visited Disney theme park, but is the 2nd most visited tourist denstination in Paris, with approximately 11 million visitors annually.

Disneyland Paris

Built 10 years after the original theme park in the US, Disneyland Paris has seen approximately 320 million visitors since its gates first opened in 2002. There are an average 10 million visitors per year, making it the 9th most visited Disney theme park globally, and the 3rd most visited attraction in Paris.
